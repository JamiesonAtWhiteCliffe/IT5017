Northland, New Zealand’s northernmost region, is renowned for its subtropical climate, rich Maori culture, and scenic coastlines. With a population of approximately 200,000, Northland stretches from Auckland up to Cape Reinga, the northern tip of the country. Auckland, New Zealand’s largest city, is a vibrant urban center located in the North Island. With a population of around 1.7 million, it’s a multicultural metropolis known for its bustling harbor, world-class restaurants, and diverse cultural scene. Waikato, located in the North Island of New Zealand, is known for its rolling green hills, agricultural heritage, and rich Maori history.
